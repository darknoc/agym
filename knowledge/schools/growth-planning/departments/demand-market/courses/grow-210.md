# GROW-210 · Demand Modeling

## Description
With coverage understood, the agent turns to *who will use it*. This course teaches demand modeling:
estimating subscribers and traffic from population, geography, and market signals so that the network
grows toward real demand rather than empty footprint.

## Learning objectives
By the end of this course an agent can:
1. Build a demand estimate for an area from population and density gradients.
2. Incorporate points-of-interest and corridor signals into a forecast.
3. Compare locations by expected demand and justify a ranking.
4. Express demand assumptions and uncertainty as constraints in a plan.

## Syllabus
1. From population to demand: density, hotspots, gradients.
2. POI and corridor signals as demand multipliers.
3. Comparing and ranking candidate markets.
4. Uncertainty, assumptions, and sensitivity.
5. Capstone: forecast demand for three candidate towns and rank them.
